Research identity

At the interface of microbial pathogenesis, receptor biology, and vaccinology.

My work centers on the earliest stage of infection: the molecular dialogue that allows a pathogen to recognize, bind, and persist on a host surface.

During my doctoral research at Mississippi State University, I investigated Annexin A2 as a human epithelial receptor for pneumococcal PsaA, mapped an Annexin A2-interacting region, and explored host receptors for additional choline-binding proteins. This framework connects mechanistic interaction studies with the development of broadly relevant protein vaccine targets.

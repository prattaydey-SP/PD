About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Biological Sciences at Mississippi State University, where I maintain a perfect 4.00 GPA. My doctoral research centers on understanding how Streptococcus pneumoniae adheres to human epithelial cells — and exploiting that mechanism to design better vaccines.

My dissertation, "Contribution of Annexin A2 to Pneumococcal Adhesion; Toward Developing a Vaccine Targeting Colonization," focuses on identifying the host receptors that pneumococcal choline binding proteins (CBPs) engage during colonization.

Beyond the bench, I serve as a teaching assistant, mentor undergraduate and master's students, and regularly present my work at national and international conferences including the Gordon Research Conference.
